About Robert Frost

Robert Frost was an American poet in the late 1800s and early 1900s. His most famous poems were written when he was a middle-aged farmer in Derry, New Hampshire. He would later leave the profession and begin teaching English and writing classes across the country. Though he never graduated from college, by the time he died, Robert Frost had received over 40 honorary degrees from institutions such as Harvard, Princeton, Oxford and Cambridge. He is also the only poet to receive 4 Pulitzer Prizes for poetry.
